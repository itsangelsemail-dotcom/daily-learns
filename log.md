## Monday, March 30 2026 — Preparing Your Brand for Agentic AI

**Source:** Article — https://hbr.org/2026/03/preparing-your-brand-for-agentic-ai
**Est. time:** ~15 min

### Why this matters for Angel
AI agents are rapidly becoming the middlemen between consumers and brands — they research, compare, and recommend products on behalf of users, which means Yelp's brand (and the brands of the businesses it serves) must be legible *to machines*, not just to humans. As Director of Brand at Yelp, Angel is in a rare position to shape how Yelp shows up in AI-mediated discovery before the playbook is written. This piece gives him the strategic vocabulary to lead that conversation internally and with external partners.

### Key takeaways
- **Agents favor clarity over cleverness.** Agentic AI rewards brands with unambiguous positioning and structured information — fuzzy brand language that works on humans gets filtered out entirely when an AI is making the shortlist.
- **The consumer funnel is collapsing into a single AI prompt.** Discovery, consideration, and recommendation now often happen inside one AI interaction, meaning brand investment must extend beyond owned channels into the signals AI models train on and retrieve from.
- **Brand consistency becomes a data quality problem.** Inconsistent messaging across touchpoints doesn't just confuse people — it creates noisy training data that makes your brand harder for AI to represent accurately. Governance and consistency are now competitive advantages.
